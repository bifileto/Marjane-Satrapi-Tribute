Marjane Satrapi

Writer of the Award Winner graphic novel "Persepolis"

  • 1969: Born on 22 November in Iran (age 48);
  • 1979: Iranian Revolution and execution of her loved uncle, Anoosh;
  • 1983: Moved to Vienna to scape the Muslim regime;
  • 1989: Returned to Iran;
  • 1990: Married her first husband, Raza, divorcing few years later;
  • 2000-2003: Released Persepolis, an autobiografic graphic novel in four parts and in French;
  • 2003: Release of her graphic novel Broderies;
  • 2004: Release of Chicken with plums graphic novel;
  • 2007: Debut of Persepolis movie, making her famous worldwide;

“When we're afraid, we lose all sense of analysis and reflection. Our fear paralyzes us. Besides, fear has always been the driving force behind all dictators' repression.”
